S. S. Rajamouli is considered amongst one the most prosperous film directors of Tollywood. He is the son of Vijayendra Prasad, a well-known Tollywood script writer. He was born on 10th October, 1973 in Raichur, Karnataka. Though, his place of birth is Kovvur. S. S. Rajamouli studied up to 4th Class at Kovvur and continued his studies till 12th class in Eluru. Furthermore, his brother and father are in the Tollywood industry. Thus, he worked as an assistant along with Editor Kotagiri Venkateswara Rao for a specific time period.

S. S. Rajamouli also offered his services as an assistant with director K. Raghavendra Rao and worked for a famous teleserial Santi Nivasam. Though, his seniors Yeleti Chandrasekhar and Vara Mullapudi used to get more fame than he actually did. Subsequently, worked harder, this earned him the title ‘working devil’ from K. Raghavendra Rao.

S. S. Rajamouli is commonly known for directing fictional scripts such as Eega, Magadheera and Baahubali which became the top grossing movie all across India, the 2nd topmost grossing movie worldwide, the very first South Indian movie to gross more than ₹650 crore internationally, the first Non-Hindi movie to gross more than ₹100 crore in the dubbed version of Hindi, and the top grossing Telugu movie of all the time.

Rajamouli has honored with a number of awards comprising three Filmfare Awards, 2 National Filmfare Awards, 2 Nandi Awards, Star World India 2012 “Entertainer of The Year” award and the IIFA Award. In the year 2016, S.S. Rajamouli was bestowed with India’s 4th highest citizen honor for his dedications towards Art field.
