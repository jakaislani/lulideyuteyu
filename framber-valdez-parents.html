Who Are Framber Valdez Parents?: Framber Valdez is a Dominican professional baseball player born on November 19, 1993.

He developed a passion for playing baseball at a younger age, has been consistent throughout, and has become one of the most sought-after baseball players.

At this time of writing (Saturday, August 5, 2023), Valdez is a professional baseball player (pitcher) for the Houston Astros of Major League Baseball.

Valdez signed with the Astros as an international free agent in 2015, and eventually made his Major League Baseball (MLB) debut in 2018.

He split the 2018 minor league season between Corpus Christi and the Fresno Grizzlies, going a combined 6–5 with a 4.11 ERA over 103 innings.

The Astros promoted Valdez to the major leagues for the first time on August 21, 2018. On his debut day, he pitched 4+1⁄3 innings and earned the win.

Valdez split the 2019 season between the Round Rock Express and Houston. With Round Rock, he went 5–2 with a 3.25 ERA over 44+1⁄3 innings and with Houston, he went 4–7 with a 5.86 ERA over 70+2⁄3 innings.

In 2020, Valdez was 5–3 with a 3.57 ERA in 11 games (10 starts), in which he threw 70+2⁄3 innings and struck out 76 batters (8th in the AL)

In March 2021, he suffered a fractured left ring finger in a spring training game. He returned on May 28, leading the major leagues in ground ball rate in 2021.

In 2021, Valdez was 11–6 with one complete game and a 3.14 ERA over 22 starts and 134+2⁄3 innings. He avoided arbitration with the Astros on March 22, 2022, agreeing on a $3 million contract for the season.

In 2023, Valdez agreed to a $6.8 million contract for the season with the Astros on January 13 avoiding arbitration. He was named to the 2023 MLB All-Star Game but did not pitch in the game.
